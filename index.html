What is CAD?

            
CAD (computer-aided design) is the use of computer-based software 
to aid in design processes. CAD software is frequently used by 
different types of engineers and designers.
CAD software can be used to create two-dimensional (2-D) drawings 
or three-dimensional (3-D) models.

The purpose of CAD is to optimize and streamline the designer's 
workflow, increase productivity, improve the quality and level of 
detail in the design, improve documentation communications and 
often contribute toward a manufacturing design database.
CAD software outputs come in the form of electronic files, 
which are then used accordingly for manufacturing processes.
          
CAD is often used in tandem with digitized manufacturing processes.
CAD/CAM (computer-aided design/computer-aided manufacturing) is 
software used to design products 
such as electronic circuit boards in computers and other devices.
